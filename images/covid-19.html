COVID-19

Analysis of the Spread of COVID-19

  • 2020-3-24

Coronavirus has spread to more than 160 countries and become a global pandemic. We created an Shiny App to provide worldwide real-time information about new coronavirus outbreak, including World Trend showing virus spreading; Detailed Situation with deaths, recovered and active cases; Daily Report that allowed country comparisons; Typical Country Data that people may concern about; Breaking News reported about "coronavirus" in four countries; More Information where you could find more about the virus and U.S. testing.

Cumulative Numbers of Confirmed Cases Across Different Countries/Regions

The cumulative numbers of confirmed cases across different countries/regions are shown in the plot below. It can be seen that since March 18th, the growth rate of confirmed cases in the U.S. is the largest among all the counties/regions in the dataset. Even though Italy is now the country with the highest cumulative number of confirmed cases outside China, the case number in the U.S. may exceed that of Italy within only a few days.

For more details of the plot, you could double-click on the legend to select a specific country/region, and click on the legend to add or delete a country/region. You could also click and drag on the plot to zoom-in and double-click to zoom-out completely.

Data Source: "https://github.com/CSSEGISandData/COVID-19"

Cumulative Numbers of Deaths Across Different Countries/Regions

The cumulative numbers of deaths across different countries/regions are shown in the plot below. It can be seen that since March 19th, the cumulative numbers of death in Italy was already higher than that in China. Italy and Spain are two countries that have the highest growth rate in the number of deaths. Besides, the situations in Iran, France, and the U.S. are also noticeable.

For more details of the plot, you could double-click on the legend to select a specific country/region, and click on the legend to add or delete a country/region. You could also click and drag on the plot to zoom-in and double-click to zoom-out completely.

Data Source: "https://github.com/CSSEGISandData/COVID-19"
